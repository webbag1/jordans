MJ had a successful rookie campaign being named an All Star, Rookie of the Year and bringing the Bulls back to the Playoffs after a four-year absence. Michael wore the Air Jordan I Red/white/black as he scored a playoff high of 63 points against the Boston Celtics in the 1986 Playoffs.

Air Jordan was originally created for basketball legend Michael Jordan, blending innovative design with his iconic style, forever changing the sneaker game.

Peter Moores sketched the Air Jordan "Wings" logo on a napkin during a flight from Portland to Chicago inspired by the idea of flight and Michael Jordan's soaring style.
